# What This Tool Is NOT

This document explicitly defines what the Contract Risk Triage Tool does NOT do. This clarity is essential for trust, legal safety, and proper use.

## NOT Legal Advice

**What it is**: Automated risk triage using deterministic rules

**What it is NOT**: Legal counsel, legal advice, or legal opinion

**Implication**: Always consult qualified attorneys before signing contracts.

## NOT a Legal Review Replacement

**What it is**: First-pass screening tool

**What it is NOT**: Comprehensive legal review or due diligence

**Implication**: High-risk contracts require full legal review.

## NOT an Enforceability Determiner

**What it is**: Risk indicator system

**What it is NOT**: System that determines whether clauses are legally enforceable

**Implication**: Enforceability depends on jurisdiction, context, and case law.

## NOT a Jurisdiction Analyzer

**What it is**: Pattern-based risk detection

**What it is NOT**: System that analyzes state, federal, or international law

**Implication**: Jurisdiction-specific legality requires legal expertise.

## NOT a Contract Redlining Tool

**What it is**: Risk detection and explanation system

**What it is NOT**: Tool that suggests specific language changes or drafts counter-proposals

**Implication**: Negotiation and drafting require legal counsel.

## NOT a General-Purpose Contract Analyzer

**What it is**: Specialized tool for commercial NDAs and MSAs

**What it is NOT**: System that analyzes all contract types (employment, real estate, M&A, etc.)

**Implication**: Use only for target contract types.

## NOT a Contract Template Generator

**What it is**: Risk assessment tool

**What it is NOT**: System that generates contract templates or standard forms

**Implication**: Contract creation requires legal expertise.

## NOT a Safe-to-Sign Guarantee

**What it is**: Risk indicator system

**What it is NOT**: System that declares contracts "safe to sign" or "illegal"

**Implication**: System uses conservative language ("may indicate risk").

## NOT a Compliance System

**What it is**: Risk triage tool

**What it is NOT**: System that ensures regulatory compliance or industry standards

**Implication**: Compliance requires specialized legal review.

## NOT a Negotiation Assistant

**What it is**: Risk explanation system

**What it is NOT**: Tool that provides negotiation strategies or tactics

**Implication**: Negotiation requires legal and business expertise.

## Why This Clarity Matters

### For Users

- **Proper expectations**: Users know what tool can and cannot do
- **Informed decisions**: Users make decisions with full context
- **Risk management**: Users understand limitations

### For Legal Professionals

- **Trust**: Lawyers trust systems that admit limitations
- **Integration**: Clear boundaries enable workflow integration
- **Liability**: Explicit disclaimers reduce liability risk

### For Regulatory Bodies

- **Compliance**: Transparent limitations meet regulatory requirements
- **Auditability**: Clear scope enables audit and review
- **Trust**: Honest limitations build credibility

## How to Use This Tool

### ✅ Appropriate Uses

- Quick triage before legal review
- Risk prioritization
- Understanding common risk patterns
- Preparing for legal discussions

### ❌ Inappropriate Uses

- Final legal review
- Enforceability determination
- Contract drafting
- Compliance verification
- Negotiation strategy

## The Bottom Line

This tool is a **risk indicator system**, not a legal advisor. It helps identify potentially problematic clauses but does not replace legal counsel or provide legal advice.

Always consult qualified attorneys for:
- Final contract decisions
- Legal strategy
- Negotiation
- Compliance
- Enforceability questions
